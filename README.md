# Vida Seattle -- Building & Community Feedback

I've lived in this building for over three years. As of recent, I am honestly flabbergasted at how quickly it’s falling apart and being completely destroyed, degraded, and disrespected by tenants and the 1000-guest parties they throw in our community spaces. Why someone would want to trash the very place that they live is beyond me, but something tells me it’s the same people. The type of people that:
a) Throw giant boxes in the recycle without breaking them down, and leave entire pieces of furniture by the trash can like this is the city dump and not their own responsibility
b) Leave their trash in the trash rooms instead of bringing it down to the garage when the chute is broken, sometimes causing an entire floor to start smelling
c) Leave their doors open and belongings just sitting in the hallway like it’s an extension of their apartment (it’s not). If you can afford to live here, you can afford to buy or rent an A/C, and should. 
d) Trash the roof with garbage and much worse (stains that don’t come out even when cleaned)
e) Invite way too many people that don’t live here over and then don’t clean up after yourselves (i.e. leaving trash out, furniture moved around, grills open, and much worse)
f) Stomp around and play super loud music on the roof past quiet hours. If you don’t already know, EVERYONE on the 7th floor can hear and feel the vibration of every footstep and base note. We even hear all the regular footsteps, so let that sink in for a minute…

### Wall of Shame
I’ve decided I’m willing to be the bad guy here and will be starting to put those that decide to degrade our property on blast, adding them to the wall of shame. Maybe we’ll have a weekly contest for worst offenders? If management did their job, I wouldn’t have to elect myself as the sheriff of trash town.

_And another FYI, those signs around the garage warning that you're on camera are complete B.S. I had about $700+ of gear stolen from my car recently, and they were not shy about it! They ripped (yes, it was essentially glued down) an entire giant tool chest out of my trunk and just dragged it out. Somehow, management didn’t even a capture a snapshot of this happening._

---

### To Management:
- If you don’t start cracking down on all of these things, this building is going to have very little allure left to it soon. This is the dirtiest and most run down I’ve seen the building since I moved in. Trash and property left destroyed by others sits around longer than it ever has before, so it’s really starting to “feel” like it looks to live here.
- The East side elevator is pretty much broken every other week now. At what point are you guys going to actually fix it instead of putting on a bandaid every time? Does someone have to get hurt before you guys do what’s right?
- The collection of donate-able items in the lobby should be donated, not left on top of the mailboxes in some sort of “pile”, which to most people looks like a collection of trash. If giving used things to other residents is so important to this community, we should develop another cleaner/more presentable system for it (preferably online and not in a shared/community space).
